# Cricket FAQ (Learning Dataset)

## What is a no-ball?
A no-ball is an illegal delivery. In limited overs, it usually results in:
- 1 extra run
- The ball must be re-bowled (does not count as a legal delivery)
- Often a "free hit" in many limited-overs rules (format-specific)

## What is a wide?
A wide is a delivery too far from the batter to hit. It results in:
- 1 extra run
- The ball is re-bowled

## What is an over-rate?
The speed at which a team bowls its overs. Penalties can apply for slow over-rates depending on tournament rules.

## What is a super over?
A tie-break method in limited overs cricket where each team plays a short one-over innings.
