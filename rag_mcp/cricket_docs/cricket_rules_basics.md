# Cricket Rules — Basics (Learning Dataset)

## Objective
Two teams play to score more runs than the other. One team bats while the other bowls and fields.

## Match structure (high level)
- An **innings** is when a team bats.
- In limited overs cricket (ODI/T20), each innings has a maximum number of overs.

## Over and ball
- An **over** has **6 legal deliveries** (balls).
- Bowlers generally cannot bowl two consecutive overs in most formats.

## Runs
Runs can be scored by:
- Running between the wickets
- Boundary: **4** (ball reaches boundary after bouncing), **6** (ball clears boundary on the full)
- Extras (wides, no-balls, byes, leg-byes)

## Wickets (getting a batter out)
Common dismissals:
- Bowled
- Caught
- LBW (leg before wicket)
- Run out
- Stumped

## Key terms
- **Strike**: the batter facing the ball
- **Non-striker**: the other batter
- **Powerplay**: fielding restriction phase (limited overs)
